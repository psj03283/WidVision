The WidVision Program provides a unique opportunity for students enrolled in SSI to contribute to the visually impaired community through volunteering, research, and writing.

Vision for 
a Better World

The aging population is causing a rise in major eye diseases and vision loss globally, resulting in a public health issue. The World Health Organization's World Report on Vision states that 2.2 billion people worldwide have vision impairment, with 1 billion being preventable or yet to be addressed. This highlights the need for action to address the issue and improve vision globally.

Meet the Visionaries

Sungju Park

Chief Executive Officer

Hello, My name is Sungju Park, and I am currently a junior at Seoul Scholars International, set to graduate in 2024. As a passionate and dedicated individual, I have been leading three different clubs in my school, including the computer science club, the newspaper club, and the together with blind community club.

With the aim of bringing about greater impact and positive change, I have decided to combine all these clubs into a grand non-profit organization called WidVision. Our primary objective is to increase eye health awareness and provide support for the blind community. I strongly believe that the eye is a crucial part of our body, and it is essential that we take active steps towards promoting eye health and supporting those with visual impairment.
