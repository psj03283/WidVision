about Us

SSI TWBC CLUB

Prior to its current name, the Central Executive Office was formerly known as the Youth Volunteer Group (VCGY) under Vision Care. During its time as VCGY, the department aimed to educate communities and schools on the reality of avoidable blindness and organized used book fundraising activities to support the visually impaired community. In 2023, the department underwent a name change to WidVision Central Executive Office following the suspension of VCGY operations.

WidVision envisions a world where individuals with visual impairments have equal opportunities to participate in society and lead fulfilling lives.

Comprehensive Analysis and Mapping of Braille Sidewalk Blocks in Daechi 2-dong: An Effort to Improve Accessibility and Safety for Visually Impaired Individuals

GEt Started
Assessment of Braille Sidewalk Blocks in Daechi 2-dong: An Analysis and Mapping Study

The objective of this analysis is to assess the state of Braille sidewalk blocks in Daechi 2-dong and to create detailed maps to aid in their identification. The analysis will be conducted by dividing the study area into smaller regions, allowing for a more comprehensive assessment.

According to the Transportation Convenience Promotion Act, Braille sidewalk blocks are a crucial aspect of ensuring the mobility of visually impaired individuals. It is essential to assess the current state of Braille sidewalk blocks and to determine if there are any areas that are not in compliance with the regulations or if there are any damaged sidewalk blocks that need to be repaired.

STEP 1

According to the Transportation Convenience Promotion Act, Braille sidewalk blocks are a crucial aspect of ensuring the mobility of visually impaired individuals. It is essential to assess the current state of Braille sidewalk blocks and to determine if there are any areas that are not in compliance with the regulations or if there are any damaged sidewalk blocks that need to be repaired.

STEP 2

Once the analysis is complete, a map detailing the location and status of Braille sidewalk blocks will be created. This map will be made available to a wide range of stakeholders, including the Gangnam-gu Office, city councilors, district councilors, and other members of the community. The map will serve as a valuable resource for visually impaired individuals, providing them with the information they need to safely navigate the area.

STEP 3

The results of the analysis will also be shared with relevant parties to provide them with a better understanding of the current state of Braille sidewalk blocks in Daechi 2-dong. This information will be used to develop alternative solutions and to implement measures aimed at improving the safety and mobility of visually impaired individuals in the area.

STEP 4

In conclusion, the analysis of Braille sidewalk blocks in Daechi 2-dong is a crucial step in ensuring the mobility and safety of visually impaired individuals. By creating detailed maps and sharing the results with relevant stakeholders, we hope to contribute to the improvement of transportation accessibility for all.
